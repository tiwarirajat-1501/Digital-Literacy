# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

## 8 Actionable Prevention Tips
1. [cite_start]**Never enter a UPI PIN to receive money:** Remember that your UPI PIN is only required when you are sending funds or making a payment, never for receiving money. 
2. [cite_start]**Do not scan unknown QR codes:** Scammers frequently send malicious QR codes via WhatsApp claiming you've won a cash reward; scanning these will authorize a deduction from your account. 
3. [cite_start]**Verify internship offers independently:** Always cross-check unprompted job or internship emails by visiting the company's official website and contacting their HR department directly before sharing any documents. 
4. [cite_start]**Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for your college email, banking apps, and social media accounts to ensure a leaked password isn't enough to compromise your identity. 
5. [cite_start]**Beware of urgent SMS/WhatsApp links:** Ignore unsolicited messages claiming your bank account is blocked, a package is delayed, or your SIM is expiring, as these usually contain phishing links. 
6. [cite_start]**Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions:** Never pay your college fees or access your banking portals while connected to open, public Wi-Fi networks in cafes or on campus. 
7. [cite_start]**Limit personal information on social media:** Keep your profiles private and never post pictures of your college ID, Aadhar card, driver's license, or travel tickets online. 
8. [cite_start]**Regularly monitor your financial statements:** Make it a monthly habit to check your bank statements and free credit reports for any unauthorized activities or instant loans taken out in your name. 

## Official Reporting Channels
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime, report it immediately to the official authorities:
* [cite_start]**National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) 
* [cite_start]**Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930**
