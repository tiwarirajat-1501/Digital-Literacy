### **Case Study: Identity Theft Through Fake Internship Offers**

**What the crime is:** Identity theft is a severe cybercrime where a malicious actor wrongfully obtains and exploits another person's sensitive personal data—such as government IDs or financial details—typically for financial gain or to commit fraud under the victim's name.

**How it typically happens (step-by-step):**
1. **The Hook:** A scammer sends a targeted email or WhatsApp message to a student, offering a highly lucrative, work-from-home internship supposedly from a well-known company.
2. **The Phish:** Once the student replies, the fake "HR representative" sends a link to a professional-looking onboarding portal.
3. **Data Harvesting:** To "process the payroll and background check," the portal requires the student to upload high-resolution scans of their Aadhar card, PAN card, bank account details, and a live selfie.
4. **The Exploitation:** Eager for the opportunity, the student uploads the documents. The scammer cuts contact and uses these credentials to take out instant micro-loans, open mule bank accounts, or register SIM cards for illegal activities.

**Who is usually targeted:** College students and young professionals are prime targets. They are frequently looking for internships, may lack corporate experience, and are more likely to trust digital communications without verifying the sender's authenticity.

**The consequences:** The aftermath is devastating. Financially, victims are burdened with loans they never took, instantly ruining their credit scores before their careers even begin. Legally, they might face police inquiries if their identity is traced to other cybercrimes. Additionally, the emotional distress and the hundreds of hours required to freeze accounts, file FIRs, and restore their stolen identity are overwhelming.
